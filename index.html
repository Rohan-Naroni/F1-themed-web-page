About F1

Formula 1 (F1) is the pinnacle of motorsport, bringing together cutting-edge technology, elite drivers, and legendary teams to compete at the highest level of racing. Founded in 1950, F1 has become a global phenomenon, with races taking place on iconic circuits around the world, from Monaco to Silverstone. The series features high-speed cars, capable of reaching speeds over 230 mph, and utilizes some of the most advanced engineering in the world. Teams like Ferrari, Mercedes, Red Bull Racing, and McLaren, and drivers such as Michael Schumacher, Ayrton Senna, and Lewis Hamilton have cemented their places in history through their achievements in the sport. The competition is not just about speed but strategy, precision, and innovation. Each race involves complex pit stops, tire strategies, and constant data analysis, making it a sport of both skill and intelligence. The continuous technological advancements in F1 influence car design, safety measures, and even technologies in everyday vehicles. Formula 1 remains a sport of excellence, combining athleticism, science, and spectacle, captivating millions of fans worldwide with every season.

Formula 1 Cars

Ferrari

For many, Ferrari and Formula 1 racing have become inseparable. The only team to have competed in every season since the world championship began, the Prancing Horse has grown from the humble dream of founder Enzo Ferrari to become one of the most iconic and recognised brands in the world. Success came quickly through the likes of Alberto Ascari and John Surtees, and continued – in amongst leaner times – with Niki Lauda in the 1970s and then Michael Schumacher in the 2000s, when Ferrari claimed a then unprecedented five consecutive title doubles, securing their status as the most successful and decorated team in F1 history...

McLaren

Since entering the sport in 1966 under the guidance and restless endeavour of eponymous founder Bruce, McLaren's success has been nothing short of breathtaking. Five glittering decades have yielded countless victories, pole positions and podiums, not to mention nine constructors' championships. What's more, some of the sport's greatest drivers made their names with the team, including Emerson Fittipaldi, Ayrton Senna, Mika Hakkinen and Lewis Hamilton...

Aston Martin

Aston Martin’s original Formula 1 foray – over half a century ago – lasted just five races. This time, though, it’s serious. This F1 squad are no strangers to success, having won in their original guise of Jordan and most recently as Racing Point in 2020. Renowned for their ability to punch above their weight, and now with a two-time champion leading their driver line-up - and F1's most famous designer coming onboard in 2025 - Aston Martin are very much a team to watch…

Alpine

Alpine may be a relatively new name to Formula 1, but Renault’s famous sportscar arm has plenty of motorsport heritage. The 2021 rebrand of the team marked the next step in Renault’s F1 revival, begun in 2016 with the takeover of the then-Lotus squad. Already race winners in their new guise, regular podiums and a tilt at the title must be their next target…
